Preamble

XPS has developed an Information Security Program (“xnISP”) which is comprised of Policies, Processes, and Standards. The Program is implemented to protect XPS against current and emerging security threats that could cause an information security incident. XPS has legal obligations which must be clearly understood and addressed accordingly.

Purpose

This Policy will ensure the controls documented under the xnISP are working effectively and efficiently.

Scope

This policy applies to anyone who works for XPS, including permanent employees, fixed term employees, agency workers, temporary staff, consultants or contractors and third parties ("Data Users"), that come into contact with information held, generated or distributed by XPS.

Controls

Compliance with the XPS Information Security Program (xnISP)

  1. xnISP compliance assessments must be conducted by qualified staff and take place on a regular basis. Responsibility for the execution of compliance assessments belongs with the Group Technical Infrastructure Director (or delegate).
  2. All compliance assessment activities must undergo proper planning and execution, including:
    1. Identifying the assessment scope
    2. Minimising any disruption to business operations.
    3. Agreeing on all compliance assessment activities and objectives with management.
    4. Identifying resource and skill needs for any technical tasks.
  3. The Group Technical Infrastructure Director ( or delegate) must ensure that all compliance assessment activities are appropriately planned, logged, and monitored via a documented compliance assessment plan.
  4. Compliance assessments must be conducted which involve procedural and technical reviews for ensuring compliance with the xnISP. Compliance assessments must be conducted at least annually and performed by individuals who are not involved in those activities being audited.
  5. All compliance deviations should be recorded and considered as part of the risk management process and classified as “Sensitive”.

  6. A detailed preventive and corrective action plan must be also documented and accompany each assessment finding.

Compliance with legal and regulatory frameworks

  1. All applicable legal regulations must be documented and defined by the XPS legal team. The appropriate XPS Department is responsible for implementing appropriate xnISP security controls based on the regulatory framework. It is the Department's responsibility to ensure compliance to the identified regulations.

The XPS Technical Services and legal team are responsible for identifying regulatory compliance requirements for data protection, privacy, and information security.

  1. An XPS Data Protection Officer must be assigned to ensure compliance to all legal regulations regarding Personal Identifiable Information (PII).
  2. All software used by XPS must be appropriately licensed and in compliance with software copyright agreements.
  3. XPS must ensure standards for record retention, storage, handling, and disposal are implemented for all information covered under legal or regulatory statutes.
  4. The retention schedule of information must be defined and disseminated. The retention schedule must contain, but is not limited to:
    1. Type of information.
    2. Inventory of sources for this type of information.
    3. Record retention time periods.

It is the responsibility of information owners to work with the legal team to determine proper record retention schedules and procedures, and work with the Technical Services Department to meet any security related regulatory requirements.

Control exceptions

Policy exceptions must be formally documented and presented for review and sign-off by the Group Technical Infrastructure Director . All such exceptions are valid for a period of no longer than one year, upon which they will be subject to further review and formal acceptance.

Enforcement

Violation of this policy may result in the termination of access and disciplinary action which may include termination for XPS employees, contractors and third party contracts and may result in further legal or criminal action.

Policy review, approval and history

This section details the initial review, approval and ongoing revision history of this Policy. A review of this Policy will be managed by the Responsible Officer on an annual basis.
