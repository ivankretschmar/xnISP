Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to standardise the meaning of obsolete, unusable, and surplus Assets and to outline XPS' treatment of such Assets. For Assets identified as being obsolete, unusable, or surplus, this policy provides direction for initiating the Asset Disposal process.

Scope

This policy is limited to IT Assets as defined in the ISP Glossary. This is an internal company policy applicable to all employees of XPS.

Controls

When disposing of an Asset, the following options (in order of preference) are to be evaluated before

choosing the one that is believed to be the most beneficial to XPS:

  1. Transfer to another business unit within XPS, including the transfer of parts upon deconstruction of an Asset;
  2. Trade-in on a successor asset;
  3. Sale through a competitive bid process;
  4. Auction or advertised sale;
  5. Deconstruction into saleable or recyclable parts;
  6. Destruction or scrap through approved vendors.

Roles and responsibilities

Business units

It is each department manager’s duty to identify Assets that are unusable, obsolete, or surplus within

his or her area of responsibility. The business unit is responsible for:

  1. Notifying the Technical Services Department upon identifying an Unusable Asset, an Obsolete Asset, or a Surplus Asset, unless a procedure for disposal has been pre-approved. The notification must contain details of the Asset and the applicable authorisation recorded on a Request for Disposal form; and
  2. Notifying the Finance Department about the Asset Disposal by providing a copy of the completed Request for Disposal form.

Technical services

Upon notification of the existence of an Unusable Asset, an Obsolete Asset, or a Surplus Asset, the Technical Services Department is responsible for:

  1. Determining the appropriate means of Disposal to be used for tangible Assets, while aligning with XPS' commitment to environmental sustainability.
  2. Determining if it is necessary to obtain Senior Management involvement prior to Disposal; and
  3. Providing direction to assist with coordination of the Disposal.

Finance

Upon notification of the existence of an Unusable Asset, an Obsolete Asset, or a Surplus Asset, the Finance Department is responsible for:

  1. Appropriately accounting for an Asset’s Disposal, in accordance with reporting standards;
  2. Creating an invoice for a third party where Assets have been Disposed of through re-sale;
  3. Annually reconciling its Disposal Request Form records for the year with Procurement copies; and
  4. Meeting annually with the Technical Services Department identify Asset owners and gather Asset Disposal information.

Control exceptions

As per the Information Security Policies, any policy exceptions must be formally documented and presented for review and sign-off by the Group Technical Infrastructure Director . All such exceptions are valid for a period of no longer than one year, upon which they will be subject to further review and formal acceptance.

Policy enforcement

Violation of this policy may result in the termination of access and/or disciplinary action which may include termination for XPS employees, contractors and third party contracts and may result in further legal or criminal action.
