Preamble

Responsible management of IT assets is a fundamental requirement of any corporate Information Security Program. Xn protel System ("XPS") shall employ robust IT asset management processes to ensure IT assets are identified, inventoried, maintained and eventually destroyed.

In the context of this Policy, an IT asset is any XPS owned or managed data link, device, operating system, application, database or middleware service that connects to the XPS enterprise network.

Purpose

This Policy defines and sets out how IT assets must be managed, addressing the controls that account for and maintain an accurate asset inventory. It also standardises the meaning of obsolete, unusable, and surplus Assets and to outlines XPS' treatment of such Assets. For Assets identified as being obsolete, unusable, or surplus, this policy provides direction for initiating the Asset Disposal process.

Scope

This Policy is limited to the IT Assets as defined above. This is an internal company Policy and applies to anyone who works for XPS, including permanent employees, fixed term employees, agency workers, temporary staff, consultants or contractors and third parties ("Data Users"). This document does not address information assets such as data or policy, procedure or standards documentation.

Controls

Asset management

  1. System owners must identify and maintain accurate details of assets in a central asset Inventory. The asset inventory must include (where applicable):
    1. the system owner;
    2. the business owner;
    3. a unique asset identifier (e.g. bar code) and location;
    4. a description of the business and technical purpose of the asset;
    5. other information such as vendor name, model number, serial number, code or firmware version, host name or application (e.g. FQDN), IP address, circuit reference or license number;
    6. security zone, as per the "Network security policy" on page 1; and
    7. a risk classification (based on by the risk classification questionnaire).
  2. The IT asset inventory must be updated when:
    1. an asset is commissioned e.g. brought into production;
    2. a break-fix or upgrade situation involving the replacement of an asset i.e. infrastructure module replacement where the hardware and therefore serial number has changed;
    3. code or version change owing to upgrade or patch; or
    4. an asset is decommissioned (i.e. removed from a production environment).
  3. The IT asset inventory must be reconciled at least annually.
  4. Access to the IT asset inventory is limited to authorised staff who have a valid business need.

Asset disposal

When disposing of an Asset, the following options (in order of preference) are to be evaluated before

choosing the one that is believed to be the most beneficial to XPS:

  1. Transfer to another business unit within XPS, including the transfer of parts upon deconstruction of an Asset.
  2. Trade-in on a successor asset.
  3. Sale through a competitive bid process.
  4. Auction or advertised sale.
  5. Deconstruction into saleable or recyclable parts.
  6. Destruction or scrap through approved vendors.

Responsibilities

System Owners

  1. Ensure that all in-scope Assets are addressed in accordance with the Information Security Program, specifically:
    1. Document and manage the Asset as per the IT asset management section of this document.
    2. Identify, deploy, monitor and report the effectiveness of information security control’s associated with the assets across in scope information security standards.
    3. Identify, assess, treat and review risks for their assets.
    4. Report security incidents and perceived risks affecting assets to the Technical Services Department.
    5. Ensure asset-resident data is destroyed before asset disposal or re-purposed in line with Data Handling Guidelines (Destruction of Data).
  2. Protect IT assets according to the requirements specified by Business Owners.
  3. Identify Assets that are unusable, obsolete or surplus with his or her area of responsibility.
  4. Record the details of any unusable, obsolete or surplus Assets in a Request for Disposal form.
  5. Notify Business Owners of any unusable, obsolete or surplus Assets by providing a copy of the completed Request for Disposal form.
  6. Determe the appropriate method for Asset disposal while aligning with XPS' commitment to environmental sustainability.
  7. Assist with the coordination of Asset disposal.

Business Owners

  1. Nominate, define and document the responsibilities of appointed System Owners.
  2. Upon notification of the existence of an Unusable Asset, an Obsolete Asset, or a Surplus Asset, the Business Owner is responsible for:
    1. accounting for an Asset's disposal in accordance with recognised reporting standards;
    2. generating invoices for Assets have been disposed of through re-sale to third-parties;
    3. the reconciliation of Disposal Request Form records against procurement records; and
    4. meeting with the Technical Services Department to identify Asset owners and gather Asset Disposal information on an annual basis.

Data classification

  1. Data owners must classify the data being processed, stored or transmitted in line with the "Data classification policy" on page 1.
  2. Based on Data Classification, System Owners must implement appropriate Data Handling controls to maintain Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability of XPS data.

Control exceptions

As per the Information Security Policies, any policy exceptions must be formally documented and presented for review and sign-off by the Group Technical Infrastructure Director . All such exceptions are valid for a period of no longer than one year, upon which they will be subject to further review and formal acceptance.

Enforcement

Violation of this policy may result in the termination of access and/or disciplinary action which may include termination for XPS employees, contractors and third party contracts and may result in further legal or criminal action.
