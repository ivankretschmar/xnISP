Purpose

User access shall be provided according to the principles of “least privilege” and "need to know" required for achieving the desired function. The purpose of this Policy is to set out the rules under which access to XPS information systems are provided, controlled and managed.

Access controls prevent malicious individuals from gaining unauthorised access to XPS systems and compromising sensitive data. Unauthorised access carries with it the potential to adversely impact internal business operations, damage customer confidence, negatively affect sales and result in financial penalties.

Scope

This policy affects all Information Systems which include operating systems, applications, databases, IT services, and network equipment.

This policy applies to anyone who works for XPS, including permanent employees, fixed term employees, agency workers, temporary staff, consultants or contractors and third parties ("Data Users"), that come into contact with information held, generated or distributed by XPS.

Controls

Logical access

  1. Access controls to all IT system components shall be implemented via automated access control systems that are configured to enforce privileges assigned to individuals based on job classification and function as well as a default ‘deny all’ setting.
  2. Account creation shall follow a documented process that includes procedures for access approval by the relevant information owner.
  3. Access to information assets shall be authorised and follow the principles of least privilege and need-to-know.
  4. Account creation and the process of account authorisation should be auditable.
  5. Accounts creation, addition, deletion and modification of user ID’s, credentials and other identified objects, shall be managed via the "Change control policy" on page 1. These changes should only be completed by staff within the IT Infrastructure and Application Development departments.
  6. First time passwords and password resets, should be set to a unique value for each user and require changing after first use.
  7. Where a request to change user access permissions is not made face-to-face (e.g. requests made over the telephone), the requester's identity should be verified before any changes can be applied. Acceptable methods may include verification of the users DoB, address or other pre-defined security questions.
  8. Information owners should identify requirements for access privileges based upon an individual’s job classification and function.
  9. Information owners should establish levels of access permissions granted for all user classifications.
  10. Management approval of access shall be documented and shall include listings of specific privileges approved.
  1. Every user shall use a unique user account and a complex, personal, secret password for access to XPS information systems and networks. Systems and applications shall authenticate using a password or token entry.
  2. When staff employment is terminated, user access to systems in use within XPS, shall be revoked immediately.
  3. User accounts that have been inactive for a period of 90-days, shall be removed or disabled.
  4. Repeated access attempts to systems in use within XPS, shall be restricted to 6 attempts. The lockout duration shall be restricted to either 30 minutes or when the administrator unlocks the account.
  5. Sessions that have been idle for 15-minutes shall automatically lock and require the terminal or session to be re-authenticated.
  6. Authentication procedures and policies shall be documented and communicated to all users who have access to XPS cloud-hosted systems. Communication shall not be solely verbal.

Usage policies for critical technologies

This Control prevents the use of unauthorised technologies to gain access onto XPS corporate networks. Examples of critical technologies include, but are not limited to, remote access and wireless technologies, laptops, tablets, removable electronic media, e-mail usage and Internet usage.

  1. Use of critical technologies such as remote access, wireless access, email and internet access shall be explicitly and formally approved by authorised management.
  2. Procedures shall be in place for the use of such critical technologies, including authentication mechanisms, such as User ID and Password, or hardware and software token authentication.
  3. Remote access technologies for vendors and business partners shall be activated only when needed for business purposes, monitored whilst in use and immediately deactivated after use.
  4. As part of the IT system inventory, a list shall be maintained of all devices authorised to access XPS cloud-hosted systems, which shall be generated from an accurate and verifiable method to readily determine the following information, which is auditable:
    1. Device owner;
    2. Owner contact information; and
    3. Device purpose.

Physical access

  1. Appropriate facility entry controls shall exist to limit and monitor physical access to XPS cloud-hosted systems. Access shall be controlled with badge readers or other devices, including authorized badges, and lock and key.
  2. Requests for physical access to data centres are subject to approval by an agreed group of IT Management.
  3. Video cameras and other access control mechanisms shall be in place to monitor individual access at all entry and exit points to all data centres, server rooms, or any area that houses systems that store, process or transmit sensitive data.
  4. Access control mechanisms shall be protected from tampering or disabling.
  5. Data collected from any access control mechanism shall be reviewed periodically and correlated with other entries.
  6. Data collected from any access control mechanism shall be stored for at least three (3) months, unless otherwise restricted by law.
  7. Physical access to publicly accessible network jacks, wireless access points, gateways, handheld devices, networking and communications hardware, and telecommunication lines shall be restricted.
  1. Publicly accessible network jacks are enabled only when needed by authorized onsite personnel; otherwise, visitors are escorted at all times in areas with active network jacks.
  2. Physical entry and exit log files shall be maintained for recording all visitor access to facilities, data centres and other sensitive areas. The log shall include visitor’s name, the firm represented and the name of employee authorising physical access. Physical visitor access logs shall be retained for at least 3 months.

Escort of Personnel

  1. Only authorised personnel are allowed unescorted access to areas wheresensitive data is processed or maintained.
  2. Individuals not specifically authorised, including contractors and vendors, are considered visitors.
  3. All visitors, including contractors and vendors, are required to be escorted while in the data centre and other areas where sensitive data is processed, transmitted or stored.
  4. In areas where sensitive data is accessible, procedures shall exist to easily distinguish onsite personnel and visitors.
  5. All personnel and all visitors shall be assigned an identification badge prior to entering restricted areas where sensitive data is accessible.
  6. Employees should exercise caution to prevent tailgaters from gaining access to any company facility (‘tailgating’ is the act of an unauthorised person following someone into a restricted area).
  7. Access cards and/or keys shall not be shared or loaned to others.
  8. Access to restricted areas where sensitive data is accessible will be reviewed on a quarterly basis.
  9. Visitor badges shall have a defined expiration date and clearly identify the wearer as a visitor.
  10. All badges shall be surrendered and access be revoked on the last day of engagement or employment.

Control exceptions

Policy exceptions shall be formally documented and presented for review and sign-off by the Group Technical Infrastructure Director . All such exceptions are valid for a period of no longer than one year, upon which they will be subject to further review and formal acceptance.

Enforcement

Violation of this policy may result in the termination of access and disciplinary action which may include termination for XPS employees, contractors and third party contracts and may result in further legal or criminal action
