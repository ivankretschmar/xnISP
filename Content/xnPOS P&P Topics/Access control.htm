Purpose

The purpose of the Access Control Policy is to reduce XPS exposure to unauthorised access by providing direction on the access controls that must be deployed as a minimum to protect corporate assets.

Scope

This policy affects all systems and resources within or with access to XPS hosted application environment.

It is the responsibility of the Group Technical Infrastructure Director to ensure that the appropriate protective measures are taken and maintained to manage these risks on their supported systems in line with XPS corporate policies and associated procedures.

Preamble

Without having strong access controls in place, malicious individuals may gain unauthorised access to XPS systems and compromise sensitive data. This not only adversely impacts internal business operations, but reduces customer confidence, negatively affects sales and may result in financial penalties.

As part of a layered approach to security, access control policies are established to restrict physical access and logical access to authorised personnel, using only authorised devices, with the minimum access permissions necessary to perform their required job function.

Controls

Logical access

Access controls to all PCI system components must be implemented via automated access control systems that are configured to enforce privileges assigned to individuals based on job classification and function as well as a default ‘deny all’ setting.

Account creation must follow a documented process that includes procedures for access approval by the relevant information owner.

Access to information assets must be authorised and follow the principles of least privilege and need-to-know.

Account creation and the process of account authorisation must be auditable.

Accounts creation, addition, deletion and modification of user ID’s, credentials and other identified objects, must be managed via the Change Control Process. These changes should only be completed by staff within the IT Infrastructure / Application Development departments.

First time passwords and password resets, must be set to a unique value for each user and require changing after first use.

None face to face requests to change access permissions (for example a user calls to request a password change), must have their identity verified before authentication changes can be applied. Acceptable methods may include verification of the users DoB, address or other pre-defined security questions.

Information owners must identify requirements for access privileges based upon an individual’s job classification and function.

Information owners must establish levels of access permissions granted for all user classifications.

Management approval of access must be documented and must include listings of specific privileges approved.

Every user must use a unique user account and a complex, personal, secret password for access to XN Hotels information systems and networks. Systems and applications must authenticate using a password or token entry.

When staff employment is terminated, user access to systems in use within XN Hotels, shall be revoked immediately.

User accounts that have been inactive for a period of 90 days, shall be removed or disabled.

Repeated access attempts to systems in use within XN Hotels, must be restricted to 6 attempts. The lockout duration shall be restricted to either 30 minutes or when the administrator unlocks the account.

Sessions that have been idle for 15 minutes must automatically lock and require the terminal or session to be re-authenticated.

Authentication procedures and policies must be documented and communicated to all users who have access to cardholder data. Communication must not be solely verbal.

Usage Policies for Critical Technologies

This policy prevents the use of unauthorised technologies in order to gain access onto XN HOTELS corporate networks.

Examples of critical technologies include, but are not limited to, remote access and wireless technologies, laptops, tablets, removable electronic media, e-mail usage and Internet usage.

  • Use of critical technologies such as remote access, wireless access, email and internet access must be explicitly and formally approved by authorised management.
  • Procedures must be in place for the use of such critical technologies, including authentication mechanisms, such as User ID and Password, or hardware/software token authentication.
  • Remote access technologies for vendors and business partners must be activated only when needed for business purposes, monitored whilst in use and immediately deactivated after use.
  • As part of the PCI Inventory, a list must be maintained of all devices authorised to access the PCI cardholder data environment, which must be generated from an accurate and verifiable method to readily determine the following information, which is auditable:
    1. Owner
    2. Contact Information
    3. Purpose

Physical Access Controls

  • Appropriate facility entry controls must exist to limit and monitor physical access to systems in the cardholder data environment. Access must be controlled with badge readers or other devices, including authorized badges, and lock and key;
  • Requests for physical access to data centres are subject to approval by an agreed group of IT Management;
  • Video cameras and/or other access control mechanisms must be in place to monitor individual access at all entry/exit points to all data centres, server rooms, or any area that houses systems that store, process or transmit cardholder data;
    1. The video cameras and access control mechanisms must be protected from tampering or disabling.
    1. The collected data must be periodically reviewed and correlated with other entries.
    2. The data must be stored for at least three months, unless otherwise restricted by law.
  • Physical access to publicly accessible network jacks, wireless access points, gateways, handheld devices, networking/communications hardware, and telecommunication lines must be restricted;
  • Publicly accessible network jacks are enabled only when needed by authorized onsite personnel; otherwise, visitors are escorted at all times in areas with active network jacks;
  • Physical entry and exit log files must be maintained for recording all visitor access to facilities, data centres and other sensitive areas. The log must include visitor’s name, the firm represented and the name of employee authorising physical access. Physical visitor access logs must be retained for at least 3 months.

Escort of Personnel

  • Only authorised personnel are allowed unescorted access to areas where cardholder data is processed or maintained;
  • Individuals not specifically authorised, including contractors and vendors, are considered visitors;
  • All visitors, including contractors and vendors, are required to be escorted while in the data centre and other areas where cardholder data is processed, transmitted or stored;
  • In areas where cardholder data is accessible, procedures must exist to easily distinguish onsite personnel and visitors;
  • All personnel and all visitors must be assigned an identification badge prior to entering restricted areas where cardholder data is accessible;
  • Employees should exercise caution to prevent tailgaters from gaining access to any company facility (‘tailgating’ is the act of an unauthorised person following someone into a restricted area);
  • Access cards and/or keys must not be shared or loaned to others;
  • Access to restricted areas where cardholder data is accessible will be reviewed on a quarterly basis;
  • Visitor badges must have a defined expiration date and clearly identify the wearer as a visitor;
  • All badges must be surrendered and access be revoked on the last day of engagement or employment.

Control exceptions

All exemption requests must be reviewed, assessed and approved by the relevant business stakeholders
