Overview

Xn protel Systems ("XPS") helps world-class hospitality companies in over 50 countries optimise revenue generation, simplify service operations and enhance the quality of guest communications.

XPS and its employees have an inherent responsibility to protect the physical information assets of the company as well as confidential customer data and intellectual capital owned by the company. These critical assets must be safeguarded to mitigate any potential impacts to XPS and its customers. Information security is a critical business function that should be incorporated into all aspects of XPS business practices and operations.

To achieve this objective, policies, procedures, and standards have been created to ensure secure business practices are in place. Information security is a foundational business practice requiring specific safeguards be in place to mitigate the risk associated with normal business activities.

XPS is also subject to many information security and privacy laws and regulations which, if not complied with, could potentially result in fines, audits, loss of customer confidence, and direct financial impact to the company. Compliance is the responsibility of every employee at XPS.

Definition of Information Security

For the purposes of this Policy document, we have adopted the definition from the U.S. National Information Systems Security glossary; Information Security is:

“The protection of information systems against unauthorised access to or modification of information, whether in storage, processing or transit, and against the denial of service to authorised users or the provision of service to unauthorised users, including those measures necessary to detect, document, and counter such threats.”

Objectives

The XPS Information Security Framework ("xnISF") focuses on three objectives for protecting information:

  1. Confidentiality
  2. Integrity
  3. Availability

This document provides the overarching approach to the management of XPS information security in general and forms the master policy document. All related policies shall be consistent with this document and support the three objectives.

Why do we need information security?

XPS requires information security to protect information assets from security threats. It is critical to protect the system environment to maintain a competitive advantage in the marketplace, to ensure profitability, and to secure and maintain member and partner trust and confidence.

Security threats originate at a wide variety of sources, including computer-assisted fraud, industrial espionage, sabotage, vandalism and natural disasters. Computer viruses, unethical hacking and denial of service attacks are examples of threats encountered while operating over the Internet. These types of threats are becoming increasingly more common, more ambitious and more sophisticated.

Security framework intent and direction

Three mutually compatible tenets provide the intent and direction for this document:

  1. Security is everyone’s responsibility. Maintaining an effective and efficient security posture for XPS requires a proactive stance on security issues from everyone. Security is not “somebody else’s problem;” as a member of XPS, you have the responsibility to adhere to the security policies and procedures of the company and to take issue with those who are not doing the same.
  2. Security permeates the XPS organisation. Security is not just focused on physical and technical “border control.” Rather, XPS seeks to ensure reasonable and appropriate levels of security awareness and protection throughout our organisation and infrastructure. There is no place in the business where security is not a consideration.
  3. Security is a business enabler. A strong security foundation, pro-actively enabled and maintained, becomes an effective market differentiator for our company. Security has a direct impact on our viability within the marketplace, and must be treated as a valued commodity. The tenets of our philosophy of protection are mutually supportive; ignoring any one tenet in favor of another undermines the overall security posture of our organisation.

Mission statement

Xn protel Systems ("XPS") is committed to preserving the confidentiality, integrity and availability of all its key information assets in order to maintain its competitive edge, legal and contractual compliance and reputation. The information security framework (comprising this policy, supporting policies, procedures and requisite management and decision-making structures) shall be the enabling mechanism for reducing information-related risk to acceptable levels.

Policy revision

All Information Security Policies are reviewed and where necessary updated on at least an annual basis.

The review process ensures that:

  1. Policies in place are still required.
  2. Perceived threats facing XPS are identified and consideration included in procedural documentation.
  3. Any new legal issues are identified that require changes in current policy or practice.
  4. Any changes to network configuration or new applications are included in XPS security policy.

A formal documented risk assessment process should also be completed annually to identify key business assets (including credit card data stores and supporting networks), and potential threats and vulnerabilities which could impact on the security of those assets.

Policy breaches

Company disciplinary procedures will be invoked in the case of staff or third parties breaching the Policy Statement and /or any supporting policies or standards.

Documentation

XPS Executive Management will provide direction for, approve, publish and communicate the merits of an Information Security Policy document. This document shall outline management's approach to Information Security as well as providing the organisation with a strong indication of the management’s commitment to Information Security within XPS.

The purpose of this policy is to communicate the direction of XPS Information Security program by providing relevant, accessible and understandable definitions, statements and explanations.

The Information Security Policy Document shall:

  • Define information security as well as its scope and importance in XPS;
  • Include a statement of management’s intent for information security;
  • Include a statement of management’s goals and principles of information security;
  • Explain the organisation’s security policies, standards and compliance requirements, including:
    1. Compliance with legislative and contractual requirements,
    2. Security education and awareness commitment,
    3. Consequences for information security violations.
    4. Prevention and protection against viruses and other malicious software attacks,
    5. Commitment to well thought-out and effective business continuity management.
  • Outline specific responsibilities for information security management.
  • Outline policies and procedures for reporting security incidents.

Policy communication and training

The XPS Technical Services Department has overall responsibility for the creation and distribution of all information security-related policies and procedures. These documents are presented to all staff when joining the company and are available on the company intranet. Contractors and third-party companies who may have access to sensitive commercial or customer data are emailed the document and asked to reply to this email to acknowledge receipt. All staff are reminded that the documents are sensitive and should not be removed from XPS buildings / offices.

Changes to, removal of, or the introduction of policies are circulated to relevant parties one (1) day in advance to allow time for them to adapt to changes. XPS does however reserve the right to modify policy immediately and without notice.

Staff are kept aware of policies via the following methods of communication:

  1. Staff meetings.
  2. Emails, Intranet or Staff Bulletins.
  3. Data security awareness training, including authentication procedures and policies, is to be conducted for new starters during induction, and for all staff, at least annually to make all personnel aware of the importance of data security. XPS shall also ensure that vendors, contractors, and business partners covered by this policy are familiar with its requirements.

Once a new policy has been introduced, following significant changes, and at least annually, all staff must endorse the IT security policies. This ensures that they have read and understood the policy (or changes) and accept any consequences should they fail to adhere to them.

Users will be made familiar with XPS Access Control and Acceptable Use policy and procedure and will be offered specialist training if required.

Employees with privileded data access: Any XPS employee granted need-to-know access to sensitive commerial or customer data, shall be given extra training to ensure they are aware of the significance of the data being held and the repercussions of disclosing it to those who have not been granted access.

Document conventions

The following is a list of conventions used in this and all related policy documentation:

  • Use of the word shall indicates a mandatory requirement.
  • Use of the word should indicates a requirement for good practice, which should be implemented whenever possible.
  • Use of the word may indicates a desirable requirement.

Hiring policy

XPS shall ensure that all new employees shall be subjected to previous employment history checks and shall provided current references from previous employers. All information gathered for employment checks shall be maintained in the employee’s personnel file.

Data confidentiality

XPS has a duty of care to its customers and will ensure that all Service Providers and Third Parties who are granted need-to-know access to sensitive commercial and customer data wiill be bound by the polcies and procedures defined under this Framework.
