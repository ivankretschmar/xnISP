Purpose

The purpose of this Policy is to set out the rules for timely and effective handling of information security incidents. An information security incident is a suspected or confirmed violation of the integrity, availability or confidentiality of XPS IT assets. In general, an information security incident is any violation of the XPS Information Security Program and related policies.

Scope

This Policy applies to all Information systems which include operating systems, applications, databases, IT services, and network equipment.

This policy applies to anyone who works for XPS, including permanent employees, fixed term employees, agency workers, temporary staff, consultants or contractors and third parties ("Data Users"), that come into contact with information held, generated or distributed by XPS.

Controls

Preparation and protection

XPS has developed an Information Security Program("xnISP" or "the Program") comprising of Policies, Standards and Guidelines. The Program is implemented to protect XPS against current and emerging security threats that could cause an information security incident.

As part of the xnISP implementation, XPS operates specific security controls in order to reduce the likelihood and impact of information security incidents.

  1. Periodic risk assessments shall be performed to identify Information risks and determine their likelihood and impact on XPS operations, and to design appropriate controls for risk mitigation.  See XPS "Security Risk and Compliance Management policy").
  2. All XPS servers and EUC hardware shall be hardened appropriately through security updates and patches and the installation of anti-virus software . See XPS "Vulnerability control policy" on page 1.
  3. The XPS network shall be securely designed and configured. The network architecture shall be reviewed on a periodic basis to ensure its appropriateness. See XPS "Network security policy" on page 1.
  4. IT systems and network component configurations shall be reviewed to ensure that configuration remains in line with the systems Baseline Configuration and approved Request for Changes. See XPS "Vulnerability control policy" on page 1.
  5. Vulnerability assessments shall be executed in accordance with XPS "Vulnerability control policy", to identify and resolve security flaws with information systems and protect against malicious software. See XPS "Vulnerability control policy" on page 1.
  6. Security awareness training shall be provided to all XPS employees and students as part of induction and as part of an annual security training program. Additional training shall be provided to staff who are:
    1. involved directly in security incident handling such as IT and security staff, to ensure that their roles and responsibilities are clear and understood; and
    2. assigned with privileged access rights to information systems or maintain access to XPS' information classified as “Highly Sensitive”, “Sensitive”, “Private” or “Public” as per the XPS "Data classification policy" on page 1.

Detection and reporting

  1. All XPS employees shall understand what constitutes a potential information security incident, how to report an incident, and what actions they shall and shall not take themselves.
  2. All XPS employees shall contribute to the management of security incidents. All incidents shall be managed as per the XPS "Security incident response plan" on page 1.
  3. Where technically possible the detection and reporting of potential information security incidents shall be automated. Examples of automated detection and reporting include alerts generated by: anti-virus, firewalls, intruder detection and prevention systems, SIEM, and protective and detective security services.
  4. A threat management process shall exist to triage and treat potential incidents that aredetected, and ensure that appropriate action is taken quickly and effectively without adverse impact to XPS business services.
  5. XPS shall ensure that business partners and third parties maintaining direct or indirect access to XPS data, information and systems are contractually obliged to notify XPS in cases of security incidents affecting XPS.

Analysis and escalation

  1. Security incidents shall be recorded, assessed, resolved or escalated as per the "Security incident response plan" on page 1. Process shall accommodate the need to handle Incidents of a highly sensitive nature that may require limited documentation.
  2. All information security incidents (real or suspected) shall be classified on receipt according to:
    1. defined incident types, identification of the potentially affected parties (internal, external and business partners and third parties); and
    2. the potential impact to XPS and the associated IT and network infrastructure.
  3. The Computer Security Incident Response Team ("CSIRT") should include personnel from XPS Technical Services, Finance, HR, Public Relations and external incident management subject matter experts.
  4. During the assessment phase the incident may be escalated to a “disaster”, triggering the "IT recovery policy" on page 1 and the "Security incident response plan" on page 1
  5. A documented escalation and communication process shall be in place to ensure appropriate stakeholders are alerted during a security incident.

Security incident handling

A fully documented Security Incident Response Plan shall be implemented, providing the CSIRT with an analytical set of operational instructions to aid in:

  1. The detection, containment and eradication of security incidents
  2. The execution of quick and efficient recovery from security incidents
  3. Preserving the integrity of company and customer data (and evidence for potential litigation)
  4. The manage of external visibility and discussion concerning incidents

Incident review

  1. A post security incident review is required for severity one information security incidents to identify the root cause of the issue, and what improvements shall be made to prevent re-occurrence. After investigation, incidents shall be re-classified (where appropriate) according to the actual impact. Incidents that are judged to be ‘false alarms’ or ‘no impact’ shall be recorded as such. This review shall be completed within two weeks of the security incident resolution.
  2. A record of all suspected and actual information security incidents shall be maintained, including all actions taken in investigating, assessing and responding to information security incidents.
  3. The record of information security incidents (real and suspected) shall be reviewed regularly as part of the information security management life-cycle to identify changes in the threat environment that demand adjustments to relevant policy, procedure and standards.

Digital forensic investigations

The CSIRT shall receive digital evidence from a variety of sources while conducting incident response and (possibly) for the purposes of digital forensic investigations. The proper handling and storage of property and evidence while in the custody of XPS staff is paramount.

Control exceptions

Policy exceptions shall be formally documented and presented for review and sign-off by the Group Technical Infrastructure Director . All such exceptions are valid for a period of no longer than one year, upon which they will be subject to further review and formal acceptance.

Enforcement

Violation of this policy may result in the termination of access and disciplinary action which may include termination for XPS employees, contractors and third party contracts and may result in further legal or criminal action
