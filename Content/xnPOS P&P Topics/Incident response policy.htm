Purpose

The purpose of this Policy is to set out the rules for timely and effective handling of information security incidents. An information security incident is a suspected or confirmed violation of the integrity, availability or confidentiality of XPS IT assets. The outcome may result in financial, availability or reputational loss to XPS. In general, an information security incident is any violation of the XPS Information Security Program and related policies.

Scope

This Policy applies to all Information systems which include operating systems, applications, databases, IT services, and network equipment.

This policy applies to anyone who works for XPS, including permanent employees, fixed term employees, agency workers, temporary staff, consultants or contractors and third parties ("Data Users"), that come into contact with information held, generated or distributed by XPS.

Controls

Preparation and protection

XPS has developed an Information Security Program("ISP") comprising of Policies, Standards and Guidelines. The Program is implemented to protect XPS against current and emerging security threats that could cause an information security incident.

As part of the ISP implementation, XPS operates specific security controls in order to reduce the likelihood and impact of information security incidents. Specifically:

  1. Information security risk assessment - periodic risk assessments must be performed to identify Information risks and determine their likelihood and impact on XPS operations, and to design appropriate controls for risk mitigation (ITSS_17 Information Security Risk & Compliance Management Standard).
  2. Information systems protection - all XPS servers and end user computing hardware must be hardened appropriately through security updates and patches and the installation of anti-virus software (ITSS_04 Vulnerability Management Standard).
  3. Network architecture design and reviews - The network must be securely designed and configured. The network architecture must be reviewed on a periodic basis to ensure its appropriateness (ITSS_15 Network Security Standard).
  4. IT system configuration health check reviews – IT systems and network component configurations must be reviewed to ensure that configuration remains in line with the systems Baseline Configuration and approved Request for Changes (RFCs) (ITSS_04 Vulnerability Management Standard).
  5. Vulnerability assessments - vulnerability assessments must be executed in line with XPS’ Vulnerability Management Standard, to identify and remediate security flaws with information systems and protect against malicious software (ITSS_04 Vulnerability Management Standard).
  6. Security awareness training - Security awareness training must be provided to all XPS employees and students as part of induction and as part of an annual security training program (ITSS_18 Human Resources Security Standard). Additional training must be provided to staff who are:
    1. involved directly in security incident handling such as IT and security staff, to ensure that their roles and responsibilities are clear and understood; and
    2. assigned with privileged access rights to information systems or maintain access to XPS' information classified as “Highly Sensitive”, “Sensitive”, “Private” or “Public” as per XPS Data Classification Standard.

Detection and reporting

  1. All XPS employees must understand what constitutes a potential information security incident, how to report an incident, and what actions they must and must not take themselves.
  2. All XPS employees must contribute to the management of security incidents. All incidents must be managed as per the XPS Incident Management Plan.
  3. Where technically possible the detection and reporting of potential information security incidents must be automated. Examples of automated detection and reporting include alerts generated by:
    1. anti-virus;
    2. firewalls, IDS/IPS;
    3. SIEM; and
    4. protective and detective security services.
  4. A threat management process must exist to triage and treat potential incidents that aredetected, and ensure that appropriate action is taken quickly and effectively without adverse impact to XPS business services.
  5. XPS must ensure that business partners and third parties maintaining direct or indirect access to XPS data, information and systems are contractually obliged to notify XPS in cases of security incidents affecting XPS.

Analysis and escalation

  1. Security incidents must be recorded, assessed, remediated and escalated as per the "Incident response procedure" on page 1. Process must accommodate the need to handle Incidents of a highly sensitive nature that may require limited documentation.
  2. All actual or suspected information security incidents must be classified on receipt according to:
    1. defined incident types, identification of the potentially affected parties (internal, external and business partners and third parties); and
    2. the potential impact to XPS and the associated IT and network infrastructure.
  3. Local (Faculty, Divisions) and Enterprise (UNSW IT) teams must coordinate with the Customer Technology Services and Security Operations Teams to assess the nature and criticality of the incident as per the Security Incident Handling Plan / Process, see Control 1.4.1), or close the incident with justification.
  4. The security incident response team (CSIRT) may include personnel from UNSW IT, Faculties, Divisions, other UNSW specialist functions such as HR, Legal, Media and external incident management Subject Matter Experts (SMEs).
  5. During the assessment phase the incident may be escalated to a “disaster”, triggering the ITSS_16 IT Recovery Standard and the respective IT Recovery Plan.
  6. A documented escalation and communication process must be in place to ensure appropriate stakeholders (e.g. senior management, ISSG, Information Security Task Force, HR, Legal and Media) are alerted during a security incident. The incident may need to be disclosed to:
  7. UNSW Students and/or academic staff if the information security incident involves material subject to Privacy laws and regulations, such as Personally Identifiable Information (PII) and credit/debit card details.
  8. Relevant state and commonwealth law enforcement agencies if the incident involves a breach of regulatory obligations.
  9. Legal advice must be sought from the UNSW Legal team before informing regulatory and or law enforcement agencies. UNSW Student Services must be engaged if the incident involves a student or the broader student community.

Security incident handling

  1. A documented Security Incident Response Plan (according to the incident’s type and nature) must be implemented which provides an analytical set of operational instructions in order to assist XPS' security incident response team (CSIRT) to:
    1. respond in a correct manner to detected security incidents including their containment and eradication;
    2. perform quick and efficient recovery from security incidents returning to either a prior state, or a more secure state;
    3. preserve the integrity of the relevant data and evidence for potential litigation (if applicable);
    4. interact with the media, including social media, to manage external visibility and discussion around the incident.

Incident review

  1. A post security incident review is required for severity one information security incidents to identify the root cause of the issue, and what improvements must be made to prevent re-occurrence. After investigation, incidents must be re-classified (where appropriate) according to the actual impact. Incidents that are judged to be ‘false alarms’ or ‘no impact’ must be recorded as such. This review must be completed within two weeks of the security incident resolution.
  2. A record of all suspected and actual information security incidents must be maintained, including all actions taken in investigating, assessing and responding to information security incidents.
  3. The record of suspected and actual information security incidents must be regularly reviewed as part of the information security management lifecycle to identify changes in the threat environment that call for adjustments to the Information Security Risk Assessment Process (Information Security Risk & Compliance Management), IT Security Policy, and/or Security Standards and Procedures.

Control exceptions

Policy exceptions must be formally documented and presented for review and sign-off by the Group Technical Infrastructure Director . All such exceptions are valid for a period of no longer than one year, upon which they will be subject to further review and formal acceptance.

Policy enforcement

Violation of this policy may result in the termination of access and/or disciplinary action which may include termination for XPS employees, contractors and third party contracts and may result in further legal or criminal action
