IT recovery policy

Purpose

The purpose of this Policy is to enable the recovery of critical XPS services in a timely manner to minimise the effect of IT disruptions and to maintain resilience before, during, and after a disruption.

Scope

This Policy applies to all XPS IT systems including, IT systems (servers, operating systems, databases etc.), network devices (routers, switches, etc.) and End User Computing Hardware (e.g. desktops), used to support and deliver XPS business applications and services.

Controls

Business impact assessment

XPS shall perform an annual Business Impact Assessment to define the business requirements for business application recoverability.

IT recovery plan

  1. Based on the results of the Business Impact Assessment, the  Technical Services Department is responsible for development of an IT Recovery Plan ("ITRP") for the recovery of IT systems following a disruption of IT services or a disaster.
  2. The ITRP should provide for the efficient recovery of XPS information systems within the predetermined recovery point objective (RPO) and recovery time objective (RTO).
  3. The ITRP shall employ appropriate recovery strategies to restore business functions in the required time following any disruption of service or disaster through an appropriate combination of IT process workaround procedures, technical redundancy, and IT recovery planning.
  4.  The ITRP shall include:
    1. Business applications recovery requirements (i.e. RPO and RTO for each businss application).
    2. A list of all IT assets required for the delivery of each business application.
    3. Documentation of all IT personnel, backup systems, resources and assets necessary for recovery.
    4. Documentation of the DR-site, such as the information about interfaces with the production site, IT architecture diagrams and IT Asset Inventories.
    5. Documentation of all roles, responsibilities, and agreements regarding actions during execution of the plan including internal personnel or any external party agreements.
    6. Detailed recovery procedures that list the individual steps to restore IT resources.
    7. Documentation of explicit procedures for restoration of IT resources (e.g., all major services and systems, manual backup process documentation, logistics and action plans).
    8. Steps to fall-back to production environment when DR-Site is no longer required (including a fall-back timeline and schedule).
  5. The Technical Services Department shall ensure that the ITRP is re-distributed each time the plan is updated.
  6. The ITRP shall be reviewed and updated where necessary (e.g. following any major outage where the recovery process was deemed inadequate, weak or where time-frame issues were identified).

ITRP maintenance

  1. The ITRP should be reviewed annually or following any significant change in the XPS IT operating environment or IT architecture.
  2. The ITRP should be tested on an annual basis to ensure its operational capability, efficiency and appropriateness. An annual Recovery Plan Test plan should be documented and include:
    1. The type and scope of test (e.g., complete loss of the data centre, loss of specific applications).
    2. Test objectives including the RTOs and RPOs to be achieved for the applications in scope.
    3. A detailed testing schedule.
    4. Roles and responsibilities for involved Departments and external stakeholders.
    5. Notification of relevant contacts such as Departments, vendors and other third parties.
  3. Upon test completion, the following additional tasks should be performed:
    1. Validate whether objectives and success criteria have been met.
    2. Determine what systems or processes failed and require a re-test.
    3. Create a Test Results Report to document the time-lines and results, including lessons learnt and action items.
    4. Update recovery plans as necessary and follow through on action items.

Control exceptions

Policy exceptions must be formally documented and presented for review and sign-off by the Information Security Manager. All such exceptions are valid for a period of no longer than one year, upon which they will be subject to further review and formal acceptance.

Enforcement

Violation of this policy may result in the termination of access and disciplinary action which may include termination for XPS employees, contractors and third party contracts and may result in further legal or criminal action.
