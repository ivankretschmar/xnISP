Centralised notification and control of security incident investigation is necessary to ensure that immediate attention and appropriate resources are applied to control, eliminate and determine the root cause of any event that could potentially disrupt the operation of the company or compromise company data.

Entities that have experienced a suspected or confirmed security breach shall take prompt action to help prevent additional exposure of sensitive commercial or customer data.

Purpose

The Incident response procedure aims to:

  1. Identify accountability for responding to Computer Security Incidents
  2. Ensure appropriate escalation
  3. Ensure effective administrative response to computer security incidents
  4. Streamline the response process
  5. Secure and protect data in order to minimise the organisational impact of a computer security incident

Scope

This procedural document makes reference to any malicious attempt (successful or unsuccessful), to negatively impact the confidentiality or integrity of corporate (including customer) data or XPS cloud-hosted services.

Security incident types include (but are not limited to):

  1. Malicious code attacks, i.e. attacks by programs such as Viruses, Trojan Horse programs, Worms, Rootkits and Scripts to gain privileges, capture passwords or modify audit logs to hide unauthorised activity
  2. Unauthorised access including unauthorised users logging into a legitimate account, unauthorised access to files and directories, unauthorised operation of “sniffer” devices or rogue wireless access points
  3. Disruption of services including the erasing of programs or data, mail spamming, denial of service attacks or altering system functionality
  4. Misuse involving the utilisation of computer resources for other than official purposes;
  5. espionage
  6. Hoaxes (generally an e-mail warning of a non-existent virus)
  7. Unusual events including erratic and persistent unusual system behaviour on desktops, servers or the company network, inexplicable lock out of user accounts or the existence of a strange process running and accumulating large amounts of CPU time

This document applies to all XPS staff with Computer Security Incident Response Team ("CSIRT") responsibilities including, but not limited to; collection and preservation of digital evidence, incident response and investigations.

Roles and responsibilities

All members of the CSIRT are responsible for knowing and following the information contained in this Incident Response Plan. Members are required to read and obtain necessary clarification of these policies and procedures. Members are required to sign a statement of receipt acknowledging that they have received a copy of this Plan and understand that they are responsible to read and become familiar with its contents. All members are responsible for keeping informed about revisions to this manual. All revisions will be provided to members by the CSIRT Coordinator.

Incident response procedures

Image: Incident response process

Incident reporting

  1. All suspected computer security incidents shall be reported immediately to the CSIRT Coordinator.
  2. Employees shall report a suspected computer security incident according to normal practice within their business unit. This could be to their Supervisor, or to a member of the Technical Services Department.
  3. Where the computer security incident involves physical security issues in addition to computer security issues, the incident shall be reported to a senior Manager who will in turn alert the CSIRT Coordinator.

Incident management

Incident classification

Incidents will be classified by the CSIRT Coordinator based on the perceived impact to the company’s resources and the type of data which could have been compromised:

  1. Minor: Incidents for which there are routine solutions. Sensitive information has not been exposed or accessed by unauthorised parties.
  2. Medium: Incidents that do not have routine solutions but are limited in scope and consequences.
  3. Severe: Incidents that involve significant personal data leakage, card holder data, compromised company data, or that impacts a significant number of users, all of which has significant consequences.

For all incidents, the CSIRT Coordinator shall:

  1. Create an incident file
  2. Identify the scope and type of incident (including severity)
  3. Notify appropriate internal and external parties (including relevant police agencies)
  4. Take corrective action as described in the guidelines for securing and preserving electronic evidence
  5. Report, as required, to the appropriate department for further action or discipline
  6. Close the incident file.

For 'Minor' and 'Medium' incidents, the CSIRT Coordinator shall:

  1. Assemble a CSIRT comprising of the relevant data or issue owner(s)
  2. Provide regular briefings to the CSIRT by e-mail at least once a day and more often at the outset (even if there has been “no change”)
  3. Write a closing incident report that is shared with the CSIRT

Incident closure

For all 'Medium' and 'Severe' incidents, a Closing Incident Report shall be prepared by the CSIRT Coordinator for all medium and severe incidents. The report shall include:

  1. A chronology of the incident and summary of actions taken
  2. the scope of risk the company faced during the incident (e.g. number of records, degree of exposure);
  3. A description of action(s) taken to mitigate and resolve the issue;
  4. A brief explanation of the basis for key decisions;
  5. Any communications sent and received;
  6. Identification of internal improvements to infrastructure and systems;
  7. Evaluation of whether the Incident Response procedure was followed and any recommendations for improvement; and
  8. Any other recommended actions.

Digital forensic investigations and evidence

The CSIRT shall receive digital evidence from a variety of sources while conducting incident response and (possibly) for the purposes of digital forensic investigations. The proper handling and storage of property and evidence while in the custody of XPS staff is paramount.

Receipt of digital evidence

Upon taking possession of digital evidence, a member of the CSIRT shall:

  1. Record the time and date of receipt;
  2. Record who delivered the evidence;
  3. Ensure the required paperwork has been filed or is accompanying the evidence (e.g. a forensic services request form).
  4. Complete an internal Evidence Form;
  5. Complete an internal Chain of Custody Form and attach it to the sealed evidence;
  6. Placed the evidence under proper seal if not already delivered in that condition;
  7. Labeled the evidence with a unique case number and individual evidence item number.

Handling of digital evidence

  1. Forensic examiners will often have the need to retrieve evidence as part of their ongoing investigations. Evidence should only be out of secure evidence storage for the time required to conduct the examination.
  2. Evidence should not be left out overnight or during weekend hours.
  3. All evidence stored within secure evidence storage shall be under proper seal. The seal shall be done in such a way that any opening or tampering with the evidence would be immediately obvious.
  4. Proper seals include things such as heat sealing and evidence tape.
  5. All seals shall be initialed and dated by the individual sealing the evidence.
  6. Sealed evidence should only be opened during the examination of evidence or when additional information is needed about the evidence (serial numbers, etc.).
  7. When opening sealed evidence, care should be taken to leave the original seal intact if possible by creating a new opening in the packaging.
  8. Once finished with the evidence it shall be sealed as soon as possible, creating a new seal which will bear the initials and date of the person resealing the evidence.
  9. All evidence stored by XPS will have an evidence label affixed to either the evidence itself or the evidence bag it is stored within.
  10. Labels shall have, at a minimum, the CSIRT assigned case number and the unique evidence item number for that particular piece of evidence.
  11. Anytime evidence is removed from the secure evidence storage, the internal Chain of Custody Form for that case number shall be updated.
  12. Each case that has physical evidence shall have an internal Chain of Custody Form generated and stored with the evidence.
  13. The person removing the evidence will indicate the date/time of when the evidence was removed, why it was removed, and the date/time of when the evidence was returned to the secure evidence storage.

Release of evidence to other parties (e.g. law enforcement)

  1. The evidence will be removed from secure storage and the internal Chain of Custody Form will be updated to reflect its removal and surrender to another party.

  2. The Incoming Evidence Form shall be updated to reflect the exact items of evidence that is going to be released to another party;

  3. If the person receiving the evidence is local, have them sign the Incoming Evidence Form, acknowledging they are taking possession of the indicated evidence items (maintaining the chain of custody). If the person taking possession of the evidence requests a copy of the Incoming Evidence Form, provide them a copy.

  4. If the evidence has to be shipped to the requestor, securely package the evidence and send them a copy of the Incoming Evidence Form to sign as well as the Chain of Custody Letter. The requestor will sign the Incoming Evidence Form and send it back to XPS.

Destruction of evidence

At times, seized digital evidence may no longer be required at the end of an investigation. In these cases, the storage device containing the digital evidence shall be wiped using approved tools and readied for re-use on a new case.

In those cases where digital evidence was used as evidence in a criminal or administrative investigation, the evidence shall be maintained as long as required in accordance with XPS policy or applicable state or federal law. Prior to destruction, approval shall be given by the XPS legal team, the CSIRT Coordinator and the Information Security Manager.

When evidence is no longer required and all of the above approvals have been obtained, the evidence shall be destroyed by securely deleting it or physically destroying the storage device it is contained on.

Audit

Secure evidence storage shall be audited by the CSIRT Coordinator at least once every six months. Results shall be documented and saved as evidence of the audit.

Retention of evidence

  1. The CSIRT Coordinator is responsible for maintaining evidence properly and for coordinating the release and destruction of evidence. At no time shall evidence be released, destroyed, or removed from evidence control without the approval of the CSIRT Coordinaotr. For the purposes of this policy, evidence is referring to both the original evidence as well as any forensic images or other data derived from original evidence.
  2. In cases of non-criminal investigations (e.g. fraud, waste, abuse etc) resulting in disciplinary action or employee termination, evidence shall be maintained until legal counsel advise that can be destroyed.
  3. In criminal investigations, evidence shall be maintained until the case is adjudicated and all appeal time lines have been exhausted. Prior to destruction, concurrence shall be obtained from the case Prosecutor.

Destruction of evidence

If the CSIRT Coordinator reviews the Case Disposition Status Request form and authorisation is received (to destroy evidence), the Coordinator may authorise the destruction of the digital evidence and the release of any physical evidence. This may include the deletion of forensic image files, exported data, forensic software case information and returned original evidence back to the submitting party. When evidence is destroyed, the following shall be documented on the corresponding Incoming Evidence form:

  1. The person who destroyed evidence
  2. The date and time the evidence was destroyed
  3. The method of destruction (deletion, physical, etc.)

Case reports

All case reports and information contained within the master case files shall be retained forever.
