Purpose

This document sets out the Data Classification Policy for the XPS Information Security Program ("xnISP"). Data classification is central to the xnISP. By understanding, and classifying Data according to its value, legal requirements, sensitivity, and importance to customers, we can ensure that appropriate protection is provided.

Scope

This Policy applies to all data or information that is created, collected, stored or processed by XPS, in electronic or non-electronic formats.

This Policy applies to anyone who works for XPS, including permanent employees, fixed term employees, agency workers, temporary staff, consultants or contractors and third parties ("Data Users").

Classifications

It is the responsibility of all employees within the xnISP scope to ensure that information is appropriately classified. The classification shall be determined by the owner of the information. This could be the creator, or the designated manager responsible for a given information asset that is storing the information.

Data Classification Description
Strictly confidential Information which is subject to external regulation and would result in significant financial impact.
Confidential Organisation internal information requiring need-to-know access control and secure storage e.g. strategic, financial, human resources information. Disclosure to third-parties only with appropriate authorisation and agreements in place.
Client documents All customer information containing personal information. Should not be disclosed to any third party unless authorised by the client.
Restricted Day-to-day internal business information which can be accessed by all employees and agreed third parties.
Public Information authorised for public release.

Where information has no appropriate classification label applied, or the creator is unsure about the level of classification, the information shall be classified as Restricted as a default level.

The Information Security Management Team shall provide guidance to help with the appropriate classification of information. It is the responsibility of everyone within the xnISP scope to ensure that appropriate procedures are followed in the handling of information, according to its classification. This includes, whenever possible, explicit labeling of information according to the Information Classification, Labelling and Handling Matrix.

Authorisation process

Where specific reference is made to authorisations e.g. for external transmission of confidential information, these may be made on an individual basis, or as part of an ongoing job function. It should be communicated clearly to the individual(s) concerned on what basis the authorisation is being given.

Authorisation granted for exceptions to the Information Classification Policy and associated Handling Procedures may be given by the Managing Director.

Clear desk and screen

  1. Each individual's work space shall be cleared of documents with classification Strictly Confidential or any Client Documents when employees are away from their desks for an extended period of time and at the end of each working day. Documents shall be placed in an appropriate lockable storage system or within a secure area.
  2. All computers shall be shut down in accordance with the operating systems' procedure and switched off when not in use. If the user is away from his or her desk for a short time then they should either log out or lock the screen.

Note: to enforce locking of the screen when the computer is unattended an automatic screen saver shall be configured to activate after 10 minutes of idle time. The screen saver shall be password protected so that authentication is required to access the idle terminal.

  1. At the end of the working day (or when leaving the office for the day) each user will allow time for the following activities:
    1. Place all documents classified as e-know – Sensitive in appropriate storage, in line with the Information Classification Policy, and Labelling and Handling Matrix
    2. Ensure that, where appropriate, the storage e.g. filing cabinet/safe has been locked securely
    3. Ensure that their computer is switched off or locked.

Information exchange

In order to maintain the security of information and physical media exchanged between the organisation and any third party, appropriate security controls and agreements shall be considered and implemented.

Where any information is transferred between the company and a third party, either electronically or on paper, care must be taken to protect this information from loss, interception or modification in line with the Information Classification Policy and associated handling rules.

Our employees shall be made aware through security awareness building activities that exchange of information can occur through a variety of means including fax, email and telephone and that precautions should be taken to minimise the associated risks with these means of communication.

Where possible, sensitive information that is electronically transmitted externally shall be encrypted to reduce the impact of interception. Alternatively, where data is exchanged (external to the organisation) on physical media e.g. Memory sticks or disks these shall be sent using a secure courier service with appropriate tracking information.

Where appropriate, agreements with third parties (e.g. suppliers and customers) shall contain the third parties' responsibility to maintain the integrity and confidentiality of our information.

Any exchange of information shall be compliant with relevant legislation, this may include the consideration of intellectual property and the Data Protection Act.

Passwords shall not be disclosed to any colleagues. If email access is needed then the relevant permissions should be granted.
